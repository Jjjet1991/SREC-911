Communications Officer & Report Technician Recruitment Process

The recruitment process can be rigorous, requiring each applicant to take and pass skills based tests and undergo an extensive background check. Qualified candidates can expect the selection process to last 6-8 weeks; beginning with the skills-based exam administered by the Center, ending with a final offer of employment.


The recruitment process includes:

  • Completed Application
  • Successful completion of Criticall Testing
  • Typing Test - 35wmp
  • Group Interview and / or Interview Panel
  • Complete Personal History Packet
  • Pre-Hire Background Interview
  • Conditional Offer of Hire
  • Polygraph Test
  • Psychological Examination
  • Four Hour in Center Observation
  • Hearing Test
  • Extensive Background and Criminal History Check
Please note: A “No Show” to any SREC initiated test or interview will result in the removal from the current recruiting process. If you do not pass the Criticall test you may retest after 90 days.

Career Opportunitiess


Report Technician

Collect information from citizens to write police reports. Document and research names, locations, and incidents to accurately document incidents reported to the report tech line.Report techs also manage the online reporting system. This is an entry level position.

911 Call Receiver

Answer and triage incoming 911 calls. Call receivers speak directly to the public to gather information about their situation. They then enter the call details into a computer aided dispatch system where the information is sent to fire or law dispatchers. This is an entry level position.


Law Dispatcher

Law Dispatchers take the information from the call receivers and relay it to deputies in the field. Information is relayed to officers via radio, computer messaging, and telephone. Enter and maintain certain criminal justice records.

Dispatchers are required to spend 18 months call receiving.

Fire Dispatcher

Fire Dispatchers relay information to fire crews in the field.They aid with CPR and pre-arrival instructions. The assist fire crews while on scene. Fire Dispatchers are trained to support crews needs for medical, fire, and alarm responses.

Dispatchers are required to spend 18 months call receiving.
