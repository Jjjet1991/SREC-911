Recruitment Process

The recruitment process can be rigorous, requiring each applicant to take and pass skills based tests and undergo an extensive background check. Qualified candidates can expect the selection process to last 6-8 weeks; beginning with the skills-based exam administered by the Center, ending with a final offer of employment.


The recruitment process includes:

  • Completed Application
  • Successful completion of Criticall Testing
  • Typing Test - 35wpm
  • Group Interview and / or Interview Panel
  • Complete Personal History Packet
  • Pre-Hire Background Interview
  • Conditional Offer of Hire
  • Polygraph Test
  • Psychological Examination
  • Four Hour in Center Observation
  • Hearing Test
  • Extensive Background and Criminal History Check
Please note: A “No Show” to any SREC initiated test or interview will result in the removal from the current recruiting process. If you do not pass the Criticall test you may retest after 90 days. Once you have completed the group or panel interview phase, and are not invited to continue in recruiting process, you may reapply for a Communications Officer position after a two-year waiting period.

Career Opportunities


Report Technician

Collect information from citizens to write police reports. Document and research names, locations, and incidents to accurately document incidents reported to the report tech line. Techs also add additional information to previously filed reports. This is an entry level position.

911 Call Receiver

Answer and triage incoming 911 calls. Call receivers speak directly to the public to gather information about their situation. They then enter the call details into a computer aided dispatch system where the information is sent to fire or law dispatchers. This is an entry level position.


Law Dispatcher

Law Dispatchers take the information from the call receivers and relay it to deputies in the field. Information is relayed to officers via radio, computer messaging, and telephone. Enter and maintain certain criminal justice records.

Dispatchers are required to spend 18 months call receiving.

Fire Dispatcher

Fire Dispatchers relay information to fire crews in the field.They aid with CPR and pre-arrival instructions. The assist fire crews while on scene. Fire Dispatchers are trained to support crews needs for medical, fire, and alarm responses.

Dispatchers are required to spend 18 months call receiving.



Employment Disqualifiers


All candidates are required to successfully pass the testing, integrity standards and pre-hire requirements set by SREC. In addition, because our staff members are a key part of the public safety system, their integrity must be beyond reproach. SREC conducts thorough, intense and strict pre-employment background investigations on all applicants to determine suitability for any position. Failure to disclose pertinent information or attempt to conceal requested information will be grounds for disqualification.

The applicant will be considered unacceptable and will not be considered for employment if:
  1. Fired, quit under duress, or asked to resign from two or more jobs in the last three years.
  2. Received written warnings from past employers for absenteeism or tardiness more than three times in the past three years.
  3. Used, possessed, or experimented with any illegal drug/narcotic in the last ten years.
  4. Manufactured, sold, offered to sell, distributed or transported for sale any illegal drugs/narcotics in the last ten years.
  5. Missed more than two days of work due to use of alcohol or illegal drugs/narcotics in the last year.
  6. Consumed any alcoholic beverages in violation of company policy or used illegal drugs/narcotics on the job in the last year.
  7. Convicted of any felony criminal offense as an adult.
